How is NIFTY calculated?

The NIFTY share index is managed by a team of professionals at the NSE Indices Limited. It formed an Index Advisory Committee that offers its expertise and guidance on large-scale issues pertinent to equity indices. The formula for calculating price index is listed below –

Index value = Current MV or market value / (Base Market Capital * 1000)


Market Capitalisation = Equity Capital * Share Price

Free float market capitalisation = Share Price * Equity Capital * Investable Weight Factor (IWF) Index Value = Current Market Value / (1000 * Base Market Capital).


To enlist in Nifty, your company has to be listed on Nifty Index and has to qualify the following criteria:

  • The company must be a domicile of India and registered with the National Stock Exchange.
  • Stocks must process high liquidity, which is measured by their average impact cost.
  • The compapny should have trading frequency of 100% during the previous 6 months.
  • It should have an average free-floating market capitalisation,which is 1.5 times higher than the samllest consituent in the index.
  • Shares which have Differential Voting rights or DVR are also eleigble for the index.
  • The firm must be enlisted on the National Stock Exchange. It needs to be an Indian company.
  • The Gross market capitalisation should be free-floating. This GMC should be 1.5 times the size of the index’s smallest business.
  • The company’s shares must be exceedingly liquid.

How is NIFTY for Share Market Calculated?

NIFTY 50 indices are computed based on a float-adjusted and market capitalisation weighted method. In this method, the level of index demonstrates the aggregate market value of stocks present in the index in a specific base period.
Such a base period for a NIFTY 50 index is 3rd November 1995 where the base value of the index is considered 1000 and its base capital stands at Rs. 2.06 Trillion. The methodology involved in the calculation of indices also considers changes in corporate actions, which for instance comprise of rights issuance, stock splits, etc. The NIFTY share market index is a benchmark standard against which all equity markets in India are measured. Therefore, NSE conducts regular index maintenance to ensure that it remains stable and persists as the benchmark in the Indian stock market context.
