About ETFs

An Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF) is a type of investment fund and exchange-traded product, with shares that are tradeable on a stock exchange.

ETFs are similar to mutual funds but trade like a stock on an exchange. They typically track an index, commodity, bonds, or a basket of assets.

Advantages of ETFs

1. Diversification: ETFs provide instant diversification by holding a basket of assets, reducing the risk of individual stock or bond investments.

2. Liquidity: ETFs can be bought or sold throughout the trading day at market prices, providing liquidity to investors.

3. Low Costs: ETFs often have lower expense ratios compared to traditional mutual funds, making them cost-effective for investors.

Disadvantages of ETFs

1. Brokerage Commissions: Investors may incur brokerage commissions when buying or selling ETF shares, impacting overall costs.

2. Tracking Error: Some ETFs may not perfectly track their underlying index, leading to a tracking error.

3. Market Price Variance:The market price of an ETF may deviate from its net asset value (NAV), leading to potential trading at a premium or discount.
